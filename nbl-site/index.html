Why Now

The Canadian basketball moment.

Canada produces more NBA players per capita than any country outside the United States. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander, Jamal Murray, RJ Barrett, Andrew Wiggins, Bennedict Mathurin — the pipeline is real, sustained, and visible to the global market.

The smart capital is moving in. In May 2026, Mark Cuban acquired a stake in the Brampton Honey Badgers, telling Front Office Sports there is “a ton of upside” in the Canadian market.

And the spring is wide open. No well-capitalized, professionally operated spring league currently holds this position in the Canadian market. March through June fits between the NHL playoffs and Canada's outdoor sports calendar. The NBLC is built to own it.

Who This Is Built For

Your arena goes dark in March.
We start in March.

Primary target: junior hockey ownership groups. You already control the arena. You already employ the front office. You already sell the season tickets and book the sponsors. After March, your building goes dark for five months and the fixed costs keep coming.

The NBLC fills those months. Same building. Same staff. Same sponsor relationships extended across two professional seasons instead of one. A second revenue stream amortizing the overhead you're already carrying.

Secondary targets: community business consortiums with operating depth, and standalone basketball investors with sports-business experience.

The Deal

What a founding franchise gets you.

01

Territory

  • Exclusive ownership rights to your home market.
  • Protected geographic territory.
  • Right of first refusal on adjacent markets and the Maritime division (Phase 4, 2028).

02

Economics

  • Share of national broadcast revenue.
  • Share of national sponsorship revenue.
  • 100% of local game-day, ticket, and sponsorship revenue.

03

Infrastructure

  • League brand, marketing, and digital platform.
  • Officiating, scheduling, and competition operations.
  • Player contracts, salary cap, and recruiting framework.

04

Influence

  • Voting seat on the founding board.
  • Direct input on league rules, schedule, and expansion.
  • Founding-owner status, in perpetuity.

Terms & Structure

  • Founding franchise fees are tiered and escalating — the earliest markets receive the most favorable terms. Contact us for the fee schedule.
  • Founding franchises are required to demonstrate full operating capitalization before opening day.
  • For market context: comparable Canadian professional basketball franchises have publicly reported valuations above $2 million.

Founding franchise fees increase after the sixth market is awarded. Applications are reviewed on a first-come, first-qualified basis.
